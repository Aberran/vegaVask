Introduction


The most commonly occurring casting defects in iron alloys, often associated with the use of cores, are eruptions. A survey found that 77% of surveyed foundries experience issues with eruptions, and 71% of foundries attempt to address this issue with anti-eruption additives in core and molding mixtures. However, only 29% consider their 'anti-eruption' method to be successful and sufficient. Up to 90% of castings exhibit casting defects. Literature provides answers to questions about what foundry defects are, their definitions, where they occur, and how they affect casting quality, and suggests that they can be eliminated by changing the core, binder, or by adding additives. The presented project is focused on eliminating casting defects, primarily eruptions and burn-ins, by adding additives to the molding and core mixture. Solving the elimination of defects is one of the most commonly addressed issues in casting production and remains relevant. There are many factors influencing the surface of castings, and thus the occurrence of defects. In recent years, products - additives that prevent the occurrence of eruptions on castings - have appeared on the market. Castings using these products should have a smooth surface, free from eruptions and other foundry defects. These products have an irreplaceable place in the foundry market because it is often necessary to eliminate defects on castings that cannot be removed due to the complexity of the casting. This mainly concerns castings for the automotive industry or, for example, the railway industry. In the case of using low-quality castings in these industries, we can speak of a hazard to human life. Therefore, it is necessary to pay attention to the occurrence of defects on castings and eliminate them, also using the mentioned additives. However, we must not forget about many other factors that contribute to the occurrence of this foundry defect. From a global perspective, it is evident that the study of additives and the development of new 'antidefects' additives receive attention mainly abroad, in the countries of the American continent.

Annotation:
A casting defect is considered to be a state that prevents the use of its functional properties. It encompasses any deviation in dimensions, shape, or appearance from the properties prescribed by technical standards or agreed-upon conditions. From a scientific standpoint, there are many factors influencing the occurrence of foundry defects in castings, the causes of which need to be precisely and clearly defined. The quality of the casting is a decisive factor for all foundries, and therefore, the aim of this project is the development of an additive that enables the production of such a casting. This project is focused on a realistic assessment of the impact of previously developed additives (estimated at 5 worldwide). Since although these developed additives are used in foundries around the world, nobody knows their direct impact, and the processes of their action in the core and molding mixture have not been described so far. After assessing the effect of these additives, this project aims to develop new additives with more precise targeting for alloys, steel, and also aluminum castings.


Key words:

Project objectives:
The main goal of the project is to describe the impact of previously developed additives and to develop new ones to eliminate defects in castings, primarily inclusions and adherences. The practical part of the proposed project encompasses the following tasks:
  1. Analysis of conditions and possibilities for eliminating inclusions will be conducted. In addressing this task, the production and quality of cores under operational conditions will be monitored, as well as the stability of the core production process. Based on these observations, optimal parameters for core production will be proposed, taking into account the foundry conditions. To fulfill this task, an optimization of the recipe for cores and molds will be carried out to meet the strength requirements during core production and storage. Within this task, all possible options for eliminating defects in the manufactured castings will be proposed and described.
  2. Achieving the desired quality of the casting. This will be accomplished through the optimization of the composition of the sand mixture (including grain size, liquid component, and powder additive), aiming to achieve a casting surface free from foundry defects.
  3. he proposed project's contribution lies in the introduction of new, original insights that will expand the current level of knowledge in the field of metallurgy. This primarily involves in-depth material research on the additives used in foundries, as well as an analysis of factors influencing the technological and economic parameters of casting production using cores manufactured with the cold-box amine technology.
