Introduction


The most commonly occurring casting defects in iron alloys, which are often related to the use of cores, are eruptions. The survey found that 77% of the foundries surveyed have problems with eruptions and 71% of foundries are trying to address this problem by using anti-eruption additives in core and moulding compounds. However, only 29% consider their 'anti-eruption' method to be successful and sufficient. Up to 90% of castings show casting defects. The literature provides answers to the questions of what casting defects are, their definitions, where they occur and how they affect casting quality, and suggests that they can be eliminated by altering the core, binder or adding additives. The present project focuses on the removal of casting defects, particularly efflorescence and burn-through, by the addition of additives to the molding and core mixture. Addressing defect elimination is one of the most frequently addressed issues in the production of castings and is an ongoing concern. Many factors influence the surface of castings and therefore the occurrence of defects. In recent years, products have appeared on the market - additives that prevent the formation of defects on castings. Castings using these products should have a surface that is smooth and free from eruptions and other foundry defects. These products have an irreplaceable place in the foundry market, because it is often necessary to remove defects in castings that cannot be removed due to the complexity of the casting. This is particularly the case for castings for the automotive industry or, for example, for the railway industry. In the case of the use of poor-quality castings in these industries, we can speak of a risk to human life. It is therefore necessary to pay attention to the presence of defects in castings and to eliminate them, including with the help of the additives mentioned above. However, we must not forget the many other factors involved in the development of this foundry defect. In worldwide terms, it is clear that the study of additives and the development of new 'anti-defect' additives is receiving attention, particularly abroad, in the countries of the American continent.

Annotation:
A defect in a casting is a condition which prevents the use of its functional properties. It includes any deviation in dimensions, shape or appearance from the characteristics prescribed by the technical standards or agreed conditions. From a scientific point of view, there are many factors influencing the occurrence of casting defects, the causes of which need to be precisely and clearly defined. The quality of the casting is a critical factor for all foundries and therefore the aim of this project is to develop an additive that enables the production of such a casting. This project aims at a realistic assessment of the impact of the additives developed so far (it is estimated that there are 5 of them in the world). As although these developed additives are used in foundries around the world, no one knows their direct impact and the processes of their action in the core and moulding compound have not been described so far. After assessing the effect of these additives, the aim of this project is to develop new additives with more precise targeting of alloys, steel and also aluminium castings.

Project objectives:
The main goal of the project is to describe the impact of previously developed additives and to develop new ones to eliminate defects in castings, primarily inclusions and adherences. The practical part of the proposed project encompasses the following tasks:
  1. Analysis of conditions and possibilities for eliminating inclusions will be conducted. In addressing this task, the production and quality of cores under operational conditions will be monitored, as well as the stability of the core production process. Based on these observations, optimal parameters for core production will be proposed, taking into account the foundry conditions. To fulfill this task, an optimization of the recipe for cores and molds will be carried out to meet the strength requirements during core production and storage. Within this task, all possible options for eliminating defects in the manufactured castings will be proposed and described.
  2. Achieving the desired quality of the casting. This will be accomplished through the optimization of the composition of the sand mixture (including grain size, liquid component, and powder additive), aiming to achieve a casting surface free from foundry defects.
  3. The contribution of the proposed project lies in the introduction of new, original knowledge that will expand the current level of knowledge in the field of metallurgy. It is mainly an in-depth material research of additives used in foundry, as well as an analysis of factors influencing technological and economic parameters of castings production using cores produced by amine-based cold box technology.
